Separation Logic

This was a Mini Project for my masters degree, for which I wrote a report.

Separation Logic is a logic in which we can easily give proofs of correctness of programs involving shared mutable data structures. The logic itself was mostly devised by John Reynolds and described in his survey paper from 2002, which specfies the rules of Separation Logic on a simple imperative language and gives examples of its usage in research at that time.

In the report I described and explained Separation Logic and gave examples of its use in current research, such as specifying a program logic to verify Javascript. I also proved the correctness of some programs that manipulate simple mutable data structures such as singly and doubly linked lists and trees. I implemented some of these programs in the automatic verification tools HIP and Smallfoot and compared these tools.
